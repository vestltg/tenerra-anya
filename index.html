Common questions

What families usually want to know first.

What happens when a parent can no longer explain everything about their child?

Most of what a parent knows is never made usable for others. When that parent is no longer able to explain it, new caregivers often have to start from scratch, missing important details that affect daily life and care.

What is ANYA?

ANYA turns what you know about your child into something others can understand and step into without losing what matters. It is a personal knowledge bridge for special needs parents.

Is my child’s information private?

Yes. What you share remains under your control and is only accessible when you choose. Your child’s story is never used to build anyone else’s product.

What is Anya?

Anya is a personal knowledge bridge for parents of children with intellectual and developmental disabilities.

It helps parents capture routines, preferences, support needs, communication patterns, and lived knowledge so that nothing important gets lost when someone else needs to step in.

Anya functions as a living record rather than a static file. Every conversation can update the profile and make it more useful for future care continuity.

Common use cases include caregiver handoffs, introductions to new teachers, doctors, or day programs, emergency context sharing, and long-term continuity planning.
