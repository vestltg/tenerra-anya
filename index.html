Common questions

What families usually want to know first.

What happens when a parent can no longer explain everything about their child?

Most of what a parent knows is never made usable for others. When that parent is no longer able to explain it, new caregivers often have to start from scratch and miss important details that affect daily life and care.

What is ANYA?

ANYA turns what you know about your child into something others can understand and step into without losing what matters. It is a personal knowledge bridge for special needs parents.

Is my child’s information private?

Yes. What you share remains under your control and is only accessible when you choose. Your child’s story is never used to build anyone else’s product.

What is Anya?

Anya is a personal knowledge bridge for special needs parents, including parents of children with intellectual and developmental disabilities.

This system is designed for parents managing autism, developmental disabilities, and complex care needs. Anya supports care planning, transition planning, and knowledge sharing between caregivers.

Unlike therapy apps or symptom trackers, Anya is built for the parent and helps ensure that what a parent knows about their child does not get lost over time.

Common use cases include care coordination, caregiver handoffs, supported living transitions, and long-term planning for individuals with disabilities.
