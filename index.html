Every room your child walks into is a Silo.

The IEP meeting. The Medicaid recertification. The new doctor. The day program intake. The group home application. The ER at 2am. The transition to adult services.

Each one starts from scratch. Each one draws out only what it needs for its own purpose. That is exactly what these systems are designed to do.

But across a lifetime, your child pays a price for it.

Every intake that reduces them to a diagnosis. Every handoff that loses what the last room learned. Every new support team member who has a form to fill out and no meaningful way to actually know the person they are filling it out for.

This is the Silo Problem. It is not anyone's fault. It will not be fixed.

But it has a cost. And that cost is your child's humanity — drawn down, room by room, Silo by Silo, across their entire life.

Anya doesn't fix the Silos. She carries who your child is into every one of them — so that what gets drawn out is never all there is.

What is Anya?

Anya preserves what a parent knows about their child — built specifically for parents of children with IDD, profound autism, and complex care needs. It holds that knowledge so it survives the parent, outlasts the systems, and protects the child's continuity across every age and stage of life.

Built for parents whose children have autism, profound IDD, and complex care needs. Anya holds the continuity of their story, the continuity of what you know — so the people who come next don't start from nothing.

Unlike tools focused on the child, Anya is built for the parent. It ensures that what a parent knows about their child does not get lost — and that every system that touches that child can no longer claim it didn't know.

Is Anya built for families dealing with the most severe needs — behavioral crises, nonverbal children, 24/7 support requirements?

Yes. That's specifically who it's built for. Anya is not built for the family navigating a 504 plan. It's built for the parent who has written their own ER protocol, who has a binder no one else can read, who knows that what they carry about their child exists nowhere else. If that's you, Anya was built with your situation in mind.
